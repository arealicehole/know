## 1. Facebook Algorithm Priorities (2024-2025)

### Major Algorithm Updates

**April 2025 Algorithm Update**: Facebook rolled out significant changes to combat spammy posts, misleading engagement tactics, and low-quality comments. This update heavily impacts financial services content.

**AI-Driven Recommendations**: The 2025 algorithm focuses on:
- AI-driven content recommendations
- Deeper engagement metrics (conversations over passive likes)
- Meaningful interactions between users
- Group-based discussions over business page posts

### What Gets Prioritized

1. **Conversations over passive engagement**: Long-form comments and discussions carry more weight than likes and reactions
2. **Consistent posting**: Users who post consistently receive 5x more engagement per post
3. **Group-based content**: Posts within highly engaged groups have increased visibility compared to standard page content
4. **Meaningful interactions**: Content that sparks genuine conversations between users

### What Gets Downranked

- Low-quality health or financial claims (miracle cures, get-rich-quick schemes)
- Exaggerated health claims or cosmetic procedure promotions
- Misleading business models (payday loans, "risk-free" investments)
- Engagement bait tactics
- Contest-heavy content

### Critical Impact on Financial Services

- **Organic reach limited to 5.5%** for business pages
- Businesses may experience reduced reach for certain content types
- Sensitive financial content actively scrutinized
- Algorithm prioritizes educational, trustworthy financial information over promotional content

## 3. Character Count & Formatting Recommendations

### Optimal Text Length

**40-80 characters delivers highest engagement**:
- Posts with **40 characters or less** earn up to **86% more engagement**
- Posts with **80 characters or less** receive up to **66% higher engagement**
- Engagement declines as length increases beyond 80 characters

**For Facebook ads specifically**:
- Paid text posts with **5-19 words** perform best

### Video Length Recommendations

- **15 seconds**: Optimal for maximum completion rates
- **30-45 seconds**: Sweet spot for educational content
- **Under 2 minutes**: Maintains 70% viewer retention
- **Live videos**: Can be longer (3x viewing duration vs. pre-recorded)

### Formatting Best Practices

1. **Use line breaks**: Improve readability on mobile devices
2. **Lead with hook**: First 40 characters are critical
3. **Emoji usage**: Use sparingly, only if brand-appropriate (not recommended for financial services)
4. **Hashtags**: 1-2 relevant hashtags maximum (Facebook doesn't prioritize hashtags like Instagram)
5. **Link placement**: Put links in comments to avoid reducing organic reach

## 6. Call-to-Action Best Practices

### Most Effective CTA Buttons for Lead Generation

**Top 3 CTAs for Mortgage Lead Generation**:
1. **"Learn More"**: Most widely used, drives most results for financial services
2. **"Download"**: Most popular for lead generation campaigns (guides, calculators)
3. **"Contact Us"** / **"Sign Up"**: Direct lead generation

### CTA Writing Best Practices

**1. Use Action-Oriented Language**
- Use active verbs: "Calculate," "Discover," "Get," "Start," "Save"
- Be specific: "Calculate Your Savings" vs. "Learn More"
- Tell exact action: "Call," "Email," "Download," "Schedule"

**2. Keep CTAs Short and Clear**
- Maximum 3-5 words
- Easy to read on mobile
- Immediately understandable

**3. Focus on Benefits**
- What customer receives: "Get Your Free Green Mortgage Guide"
- Value proposition: "Save $200/Month with Green Financing"
- Outcome-oriented: "Start Saving on Energy Today"

**4. Create Urgency**
- Time-limited: "Lock in Today's Rates"
- Scarcity: "Limited Green Mortgage Slots Available"
- Seasonal: "Spring Home Buying Season Rates"

**5. Be Polite**
- Using "please" can improve conversions by nearly 2x
- "Please download your free guide"
- Respectful tone builds trust in financial services

**6. Leverage Free Offers**
- Free mortgage calculator
- Free energy efficiency assessment
- Free homebuyer's guide to green mortgages
- Free rate quote

### Strategic CTA Placement

**Multi-Location Strategy**:
1. **Facebook Page CTA Button**: "Contact Us" or "Learn More"
2. **Post Text**: End with clear next step
3. **Image/Video**: Text overlay with CTA
4. **Comments**: Pin comment with link and CTA

## 8. Compliance & Regulatory Considerations

### Major Regulatory Frameworks

**1. FINRA (Financial Industry Regulatory Authority)**
- Requires archiving all social media communications
- Mandates disclosures and cautionary statements
- Prevents misleading claims

**2. SEC (Securities and Exchange Commission)**
- Oversees investment-related communications
- Requires transparent disclosures
- Monitors advertising claims

**3. FFIEC (Federal Financial Institutions Examination Council)**
- Published Social Media Guidance for financial institutions
- Sets supervisory expectations for social media use
- Requires risk management processes

### Key Compliance Requirements

**Regulation Z (Truth in Lending)**:
- Applies to ALL commercial messages promoting credit transactions
- Required when advertising interest rates or specific credit terms
- Must include clear, conspicuous disclosures
- "APR" must be disclosed if rate mentioned
- Terms must be accurate and not misleading

**Regulation B (Equal Credit Opportunity)**:
- Prohibits discrimination against credit applicants
- Requires careful review of images and statements
- Avoid language that discourages any creditworthy applicants
- Monitor for unintentional discriminatory patterns

**Fair Housing Act**:
- Applies to mortgage advertising
- Prohibits discriminatory housing practices
- Careful image selection (diverse representation)
- Language must be inclusive and non-discriminatory

### Content Standards for Compliance

**MUST AVOID**:
- Unfair, deceptive, or abusive advertising practices
- Guaranteed approval claims
- "Risk-free" or "no-risk" statements
- Misleading rate information
- Exaggerated savings claims
- Discriminatory language or imagery

**MUST INCLUDE**:
- Clear disclosures when advertising rates
- Equal Housing Opportunity logo/statement
- NMLS number in business profile
- Accurate, verifiable information
- Disclaimers for conditional offers

### Record-Keeping Requirements

**What to Archive**:
- All public posts and comments
- Private messages related to business
- Ads and promoted content
- User interactions and responses
- Employee social media communications

**How Long to Archive**:
- Minimum 3-5 years (varies by regulation)
- Use approved social media archiving tools
- Ensure searchable, retrievable format

### Facebook-Specific Advertising Restrictions

**Removed Detailed Targeting Categories**:
- Health conditions
- Religious beliefs
- Sexual orientation
- Political affiliation
- Race and ethnicity

**Special Ad Category**:
- Housing (mortgages) falls under "Special Ad Category"
- Limited targeting options compared to other industries
- Age, gender, and ZIP code restrictions apply
- Maximum 15-mile radius for location targeting

### Monitoring and Supervision Requirements

**Policies Needed**:
1. Social media usage policy
2. Employee training program
3. Pre-approval process for certain content
4. Monitoring and review procedures
5. Incident response plan

**Supervision Best Practices**:
- Designate compliance officer for social media
- Regular review of all posts (weekly minimum)
- Monitor comments and user interactions
- Respond promptly to complaints
- Document all compliance activities

### Recommended Disclosure Language

**For Rate Information**:
"Rates subject to change. Contact us for current rates and terms. NMLS #[number]."

**For General Mortgage Posts**:
"This is not a commitment to lend. Terms and conditions apply. Equal Housing Opportunity. NMLS #[number]."

**For Green Mortgage Specific**:
"Energy efficiency improvements subject to approval. Additional terms apply. Contact us for details. Equal Housing Opportunity. NMLS #[number]."

### Green Mortgage Specific Compliance Considerations

**Claims About Savings**:
- Must be substantiated and verifiable
- Avoid guaranteed savings amounts
- Use ranges or examples with disclaimers
- "Potential savings" or "estimated savings" language

**Environmental Claims**:
- Must be truthful and not misleading
- Avoid exaggerated environmental benefits
- Use certified program names (ENERGY STAR, LEED)
- Don't claim outcomes you can't control
